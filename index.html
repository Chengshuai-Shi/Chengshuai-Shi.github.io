Research Interest

I am interested in general machine learning research, and am actively exploring topics related to foundation models, especially in-context learning and reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF). My past research was focused on multi-armed bandits (MAB) and reinforcement learning (RL) problems, especially their multi-agent versions. I also have broad interests in distributed learning and federated learning.

News

  • 06/2024: One paper accepted to Transcations on Machine Learning Research (TMLR)!

    • Our paper “Harnessing the Power of Federated Learning in Federated Contextual Bandits” is accepted to Transcations on Machine Learning Research, which provides a novel federated contextual bandits design capable of flexibly incorporating federated learning protocols. This is a joint work with Ruida Zhou (UCLA), Kun Yang (UVA), and Prof. Cong Shen (UVA).

    • A preliminary version appears in NeurIPS 2023 Workshop on Multi-Agent Security, Dec. 2023.

  • 05/2024: New preprints available!

    • Our work “Efficient Prompt Optimization Through the Lens of Best Arm Identification” is available on arXiv, where a systematical relationship between prompt optimization and best arm identification in MAB is established. Built upon this relationship, a general framework is proposed to efficiently perform prompt optimization. This is a joint work with Kun Yang (UVA), Zihan Chen (UVA), Prof. Jundong Li (UVA), Prof. Jing Yang (PSU), and Prof. Cong Shen (UVA).

    • A preliminary version appears in ICLR 2024 Workshop on Mathematical and Empirical Understanding of Foundation Models, May 2024.

  • 04/2024: One paper accepted to IEEE Transcations on Wireless Communications!

    • Our paper “Offline Reinforcement Learning for Wireless Network Optimization with Mixture Datasets” is accepted to IEEE Transcations on Wireless Communications. This is a joint work with Kun Yang (UVA), Prof. Cong Shen (UVA), Prof. Jing Yang (PSU), Shu-ping Yeh (Intel), and Jaroslaw J. Sydir (Intel).

  • 11/2023: One paper accepted to IEEE Transcations on Signal Processing!

    • Our paper “Reward Teaching for Federated Multi-Armed Bandits” is accepted to IEEE Transcations on Signal Processing, which leverages implicit reward adjustments to guide autonomous bandit agents in a federated multi-armed bandit system. This is a joint work with Wei Xiong (UIUC), Prof. Cong Shen (UVA), and Prof. Jing Yang (PSU).
